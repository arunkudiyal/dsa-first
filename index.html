First, list out the skills to acquire:

The first thing that you need to acquire to be a successful Data Scientist is the skills required to be one. What are the skills and how will they help you hold a job? Read on...

To be a pro-Data Scientist, you must start small by learning and being familiar with:

Databases:

You must be familiar with both SQL and NoSQL database such as Oracle, My SQL, and Teradata.

Master Programming Languages:

Learn languages such as R, Python and SAS for Data Analysis.

Data Visualization Tools:

Familiarity with Data Visualization tools such as - SQL, Tableau or PoweBI will further your journey in this field.

Involve & Practise Via Projects:

One way of learning efficiently & effectively is to tale part in as many projects as possible.

Basic Knowledge:

Gain basic knowledge of statistics, probability & mathematical analysis.

Data Wrangling:

Be familiar with tools such as R, Python, Flume and Scoop. This will help you survive in this field.

Learn big data tools:

It is beneficial to learn some such as Apache Spark, Hadoop and Tableau.

Soft Skill Development:

A good Data Scientist is able to paint a picture & tell a story. Business acumen and communication skills are always an added benefit when it comes to this field.

Second, Opt for hands-on practical training:

Clean, Simple & Elegant

Similarly in Data Science, the relevance of having practical experience is priced. While it is important to gain theoretical knowledge, it is also important to push and apply the concepts through practical hands-on training. Many institutes and online sites assure decent Data Science training, and they are not lying. But what is really needed alongside is the opportunity to build projects, work on real-life use cases and wholesome mentoring when one is learning to apply the concepts. If you aspire to be a Data Scientist then theoretical knowledge in the subject is not enough and you need to gain as much work experience as you can. You need to practise rigorously and constantly. Practical hands-on training is important because this is the first work or work-like experience that you get as a budding Data Scientist. This is going to be you stairway to working as a Data Scientist int he future. So grab it.

Third, Find good opportunities:

  • PERSONAL PROJECTS: Like always, the trick is always to start small. Pick simple relevant personal projects such as customer churn prediction and market basket analysis.
    These days there is no problem that Social Media cannot solve. Keep a close lookout on LinkedIn where many budding and experienced Data scientists post about their work., projects and hackathon opportunities. You can also look at GitHub for inspiration and learn about the different projects that people are doing. You can collect datasets from sites like Kaggle & UCI repository as well. If you are enrolled in a course, your mentors can help you to find better opportunities.

  • OPEN SOURCE PROJECTS: Contribute to open source projects. You can also gain experience by sharing your code with others. GitHub can help you share your work and learn from fellow Data Scientists.

  • LOOK FOR EXCITING EVENTS & PARTICIPATE IN HACKATHONS: Test your knowledge and challenge yourself with these fun opportunities. Many institutes conduct hackathons and provide free internships in Data Science. If you are committed and self-motivated, this might be a good what to network and learn about the discipline. Also via these hackathons, you come across similar, like-minded individuals who are trying to make it in this field.
    Follow relevant pages and participate in as many hackathons as possible to sharpen your skills and transform concepts into actions. This is one of the best ways to gain practical experience during the training period.
    Many of these hackathons are paid. Therefore this could be a good opportunity to learn and receive rewards for learning as well.

Fourth, Maintain a portfolio of projects:

If you have gone via the first three points, then building a portfolio should not be very difficult for a Data Science aspirant. It is important that you focus on building a robust portfolio that showcases your work. This is important as this document will present your skills and showcase your talents in front of your potential employer.

There is always a very high demand for Data Scientists so it is not difficult for one to look for opportunities. Create an account for all professional social media sites such as Glassdoor, Naukri, LinkedIn, Monster etc.

One needs to put in some time for building a professional LinkedIn account using which one can publish the projects that they have undertaken during the training period. Once you have started on a comprehensive learning path, you have to constantly post about the projects, real-world use cases that you work for. Invest time in networking, and follow the right people and pages in order to build your networking.

Irrespective of whether you are from a technical background or non-technical, Data Science training if done properly can prove to be exciting. What is more important is the willingness to constantly learn and apply yourself in this field. Knowledge in mathematics and familiarity with programming languages is definitely beneficial. But practise is what will be a deciding factor
